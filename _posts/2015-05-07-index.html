OpenProcurement

e-Procurement is a procurement initiated, conducted and/or concluded using electronic means. Its goal is to remove barriers and open up markets in a non-discriminatory and competitive way. Accessibility, transparency of tendering process, electronic documents, detailed reporting - these are only a few benefits of transition to e-Procurement software. Especially it can help public procurement that plays an important role in state’s economy.

State authorities can be discouraged from using centralized procurement software due to high cost and licensing fees, expensive implementations, and long-term training necessary. But in the era of open source and dedicated professionals there is a solution for each challenge.

The Open Procurement toolkit is an open source software complex developed for procurement automation and optimization. Free and easy to use, it covers conducting of reverse electronic auctions and storing procurement related data. Toolkit's Data Standard was developed on the basis of Open Contracting 1.0RC. These standards were extended to ensure practical implementation of the procurement process in the Prozorro system.

Technical specifications of OpenProcurement are intended to provide functional solution that will be flexible enough to work with different standards and applications of heterogeneous nature. Python, Pyramid, Angular and other powerful open source web technologies ensure reliable functionality and secure workflow.

OpenProcurement consists of the Central database (CDB) and API via which specialized eMalls can interact with CDB. Procurement process is divided into 5 stages: Procurement Initiation, Bid registration, Reverse auction, Qualification, Completion. Once procurement is announced and bid proposals are registered, users get access to the 3-round auction. Reverse auction enables competitive environment and is especially efficient on the procurement market, since price is lowering with each round.

Prozorro

Prozorro is a project started in Ukraine that aims at implementing complex IT system of electronic public procurement. The main goal of Prozorro is to provide transparent and efficient spending of public funds and to prevent corruption through public control and bigger number of suppliers.

Prozorro coordinates the work of Open Procurement toolkit and local eMalls. Six e-Malls are already providing access to the system: Newtend, PublicBid, NetCast, Prom.ua, e-tender.biz, and smarttender.biz. What is more important, the project is open, so any eMall can join if it offers the necessary functionality.

Prozorro also provides cross-access to the system. Users can see all procurements via their eMalls and suppliers can register their bid proposals to any procurement regardless the fact via which eMall it was announced. This approach creates real competition that will lead to the procurement service improvement, as well as prevent single eMall from becoming an expensive and inefficient monopolist.

Among the procuring entities that use Prozorro are Ministry of Infrastructure, Ministry of Defence, Ministry of Justice, Ministry of Economy, State Management of Affairs, Ministry of Ecology, and many others. This solution received positive feedback from users and they agree with the following advantages of the system:

  • open code can be audited and overviewed at any time;
  • obligatory auction effectively reduces prices;
  • there are transparent procurement statistics and simple complaint/appeal mechanism;
  • competition among eMalls ensures service enhancement.
